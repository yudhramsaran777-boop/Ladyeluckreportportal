# 🎬 Lady E Luck — Super Bowl Commercial Script
**Title:** "WHERE DID ALL MY LUCK GO?!"
**Format:** 60-second animated cartoon (Super Bowl style)
**Tone:** Humorous, over-the-top, family-friendly
**Style:** Bright, vibrant Saturday-morning cartoon aesthetic — bold outlines, exaggerated expressions, comic sound effects

---

## THE CONCEPT

Everyday Average Joe can't catch a break — his luck is comically terrible in every situation. Enter **Lady E Luck**, a sassy, glamorous cartoon goddess who appears to set him straight: *real luck lives at LadyELuck.com.*

---

## SCENE-BY-SCENE BREAKDOWN

---

### 🎬 SCENE 1: "THE MORNING FROM HELL" *(0:00 – 0:08)*

**VISUAL:**
Cartoon Joe wakes up, steps out of bed, and immediately slips on a banana peel. He slides across the floor, bounces off the wall, and lands face-first in his dog's water bowl. Stars swirl around his head. The dog stares at him blankly and walks away.

**SFX:** *BOING! SPLAT! Cartoon slide whistle.*

**JOE (dazed, still in the bowl):**
> "...Good morning to me."

---

### 🎬 SCENE 2: "THE COMMUTE" *(0:08 – 0:16)*

**VISUAL:**
Joe rushes outside, umbrella in hand. It's perfectly sunny. He lowers the umbrella — INSTANT downpour soaks only him. A rainbow forms directly above the one dry spot next to him. A cartoon duck looks smug under Joe's umbrella.

**JOE (arms out, soaking wet):**
> "Why does this always happen to ME?!"

**SFX:** *Thunder crack, sad trombone.*

---

### 🎬 SCENE 3: "THE SCRATCH TICKET" *(0:16 – 0:24)*

**VISUAL:**
Joe sits at a diner. He pulls out a scratch ticket, grins, and scratches it with a spoon. Numbers reveal one by one... almost a match... almost... the last number is a tiny cartoon face making a raspberry sound. The ticket itself sticks its tongue out at him.

**JOE:** *(jaw hits table)*
> "The ticket just MOCKED me. THE TICKET. MOCKED. ME."

The waitress shrugs and tops off his coffee — she misses the cup entirely.

**SFX:** *Pfffffbbt. Coffee splatter.*

---

### 🎬 SCENE 4: "ENTER LADY E LUCK" *(0:24 – 0:40)*

**VISUAL:**
A blinding golden light erupts. Sparkles, coins, and playing cards rain down. **LADY E LUCK** descends from the sky — an animated goddess in a glamorous gown, crown slightly tilted, holding a glowing phone showing **LadyELuck.com**. She surveys Joe's disaster of a morning and puts on her sunglasses.

**LADY E LUCK:** *(snapping her fingers, sassy)*
> "Honey. You've been out here risking it all... on a *scratch ticket?*"

She gestures dramatically at her phone screen showing Fire Kirin, Panda Master, Game Vault, OrionStars and more.

**LADY E LUCK:**
> "VIP access. Top online skill games. Daily bonuses. Referral rewards. Everything you need — and none of *that.*"

She points at the banana peel, the wet duck, and the mocking ticket. They all hang their heads in cartoon shame.

**JOE:** *(eyes go wide, jaw drops again)*
> "...You're telling me this whole time—"

**LADY E LUCK:**
> "This. Whole. Time."

---

### 🎬 SCENE 5: "THE GLOW-UP" *(0:40 – 0:52)*

**VISUAL:**
Joe pulls up LadyELuck.com on his phone. He logs in as a VIP member. Instantly — the sun comes out, his hair dries perfectly, coins rain down, and even the dog comes back and sits at his feet adoringly. The diner waitress pours the coffee PERFECTLY this time and gives a thumbs up.

Joe leans back in the booth like a king. Lady E Luck gives a chef's kiss.

**JOE:** *(grinning ear to ear)*
> "Okay. OKAY. I get it now."

**SFX:** *Triumphant cartoon fanfare!*

---

### 🎬 SCENE 6: "THE TAG" *(0:52 – 1:00)*

**VISUAL:**
Lady E Luck floats back up into golden clouds. She turns, winks at the camera, and snaps — the Lady E Luck logo bursts onto screen surrounded by sparkles.

**LADY E LUCK (V.O.):**
> *"Lady E Luck. VIP gaming, daily bonuses, and the kind of luck you actually EARN."*

**ON SCREEN TEXT:**
```
🌟 LadyELuck.com 🌟
VIP Members Only | Download Today | Daily Bonuses Available
```

**Joe pops into corner of screen, still grinning:**
> "Don't be me. Be a VIP."

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**
**SFX:** *One final cartoon BOING.*

| **Music** | Upbeat comedic cartoon score; big brass "winner" fanfare at the end |

| **Sound Design** | Heavy use of classic cartoon SFX (slide whistles, boing, splats, triumphant horn) |

## 30-SECOND CUT (alternate)
